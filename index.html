The Hydrogen Storage Challenge

⚡ Low volumetric density H₂ gas at STP: 0.09 kg/m³ — the least dense gas
🔥 Flammability Wide flammability range (4–75 vol%), low ignition energy
❄️ Cryogenic trade-offs Liquid H₂ requires −253 °C; significant energy penalty
🏋️ High-pressure cylinders 700 bar tanks add weight and require advanced materials
🌐 Infrastructure gap Dedicated H₂ pipelines and refuelling stations largely absent

Source: IEA, "The Future of Hydrogen" (2019)

What Are LOHCs?

Liquid Organic Hydrogen Carriers are organic molecules that can reversibly bind and release hydrogen through catalytic reactions at moderate conditions.

🧪 Liquid at ambient conditions Handled with standard oil/fuel infrastructure
♻️ Fully reversible Same carrier molecule is reused indefinitely
📦 High energy density Dibenzyltoluene (DBT) stores ~6.2 wt% H₂

Challenges & Open Research Questions

🌡️ Dehydrogenation energy demand Endothermic release requires 40–60 kJ/mol H₂; waste heat integration is critical for efficiency
⚗️ Catalyst durability Pt/Pd catalysts are expensive; sintering and coking over thousands of cycles must be minimised
🔄 Round-trip efficiency ~60–70% is lower than compressed-H₂ pathways; improvement needed for cost-competitive green-H₂ chains
📜 Standardisation & regulation No unified international standards for LOHC purity, handling, or safe-transport classification yet

Outlook & Future Directions

  • Lower-temperature carriers — azaborines and ionic-liquid LOHCs aiming for <150 °C release
  • Heat integration — coupling dehydrogenation reactors with industrial waste heat or fuel cells
  • Cost reduction — non-precious-metal catalysts (Ni, Co-based) and reactor miniaturisation
  • Scale-up — MW- to GW-scale electrolyser + LOHC projects in Norway, Australia, Chile, and Middle East
  • Dual use — LOHC as both energy carrier and thermal energy storage medium

Summary

✅ Safe & easy to handle Liquid at ambient conditions; compatible with oil logistics
✅ Reusable carrier No carrier consumption; closed-loop cycle
✅ Scalable Modular systems from kW to GW; proven pilot plants
⚠️ Energy penalty Heat required for H₂ release; ~30–40% efficiency loss
⚠️ Cost Catalyst and reactor costs still high at small scales
🔭 Promising niche Long-distance / long-duration H₂ transport where safety and infrastructure reuse matter most
